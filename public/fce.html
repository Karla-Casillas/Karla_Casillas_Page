Course Overview

The course Fundamentals of Computer Engineering provides a comprehensive overview of Computer Engineering as both an academic discipline and a profession. The first part focuses on theoretical knowledge across three key areas: the societal role and historical evolution of Computer Engineering, the representation and organization of information, and current paradigms in information processing, management, and transmission. The second part emphasizes practical skills, including teamwork, ethical commitment, and communication, through workshops, oral presentations, and a hands-on web development project using software repositories. This course is all about helping students build solid technical skills while also developing key professional abilities, shaping them into well-rounded engineers.

Objectives

  • To understand the purpose of Computer Engineering, what is the ultimate goal it pursues and the good it contributes to society, reflecting on the role that each one wants to play as an engineer in the society of the future.
  • To know the pillars on which technological progress must be based, having a first historical perspective of technological development.
  • To become aware of the importance of the human factor in professional practice and to foster a vocation o service and a sense of contribution to the common good.
  • To have a global vision of the career, its different areas of knowledge and to distinguish the outlets and professional profiles in order to begin to question their vocation.
  • Provide knowledge on current paradigms, computing trends, data management and an introduction to web development.

Units Covered

    Part I: THEORETICAL FOUNDATIONS
    BLOCK 1. Computer Engineering in Society. - Background and historical perspectives. Definitions and basic concepts. Past, present and future of ICT. - Computer Engineering as an academic discipline and profession. Skills and knowledge necessary for the computer engineer. Professional opportunities. The sense of ethics in the formation of the computer engineer. Codes of ethics. Current paradigms and trends in systems and applications. - Software development: more than programming. - Structure of computer systems: hardware, software and networks. - New paradigms and trends: cloud computing, quantum computing, cognitive computing, blockchain, smart cities, IoT...
    BLOCK 3. Information and data. - Representation of information in digital format: numbering systems, representation of numeric and alphanumeric information (codes). - Data organization. Data models. Challenges of Big Data. Data protection.

    PART II: PRACTICAL SKILLS: INTRODUCTION TO WEB DEVELOPMENT
    BLOCK 1. Introduction to Software Development - Introduction to Web Development
    BLOCK 2. Creation of HTML5 Web pages - Basic concepts - Tags - Organization of information and layout with CSS3
    BLOCK 3. Design and layout with CSS3 - Basic concepts - Selectors - Box model - Element.
